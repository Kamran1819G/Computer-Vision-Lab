AIM

To perform and analyze basic image arithmetic operations (addition, subtraction, difference, multiplication, division) on digital images

Theory/Procedure/Algorithm

Image arithmetic operations involve basic mathematical manipulations applied to digital images to enhance or analyze their content. These operations are fundamental in image processing and have various applications

  1. Addition: This operation combines pixel values from two images, effectively brightening the result. For each pixel, the values are summed, potentially increasing contrast. Care must be taken to handle overflow, where pixel values exceed the maximum allowed intensity.
In [3]:
add_image = cv2.add(base, dull)

plt.imshow(add_image)
plt.axis('off')
plt.show()
  1. Subtraction: Subtraction computes the difference between corresponding pixel values of two images. This operation is useful for highlighting changes or differences, such as in background subtraction or anomaly detection. Handling negative values is crucial, as they may need to be clipped or normalized.
In [4]:
subtract_image = cv2.subtract(base, dull)

plt.imshow(subtract_image)
plt.axis('off')
plt.show()
  1. Absolute Difference: The absolute difference operation calculates the absolute value of the pixel-wise differences between two images. This method helps in detecting variations and discrepancies without introducing negative values, making it useful for comparative analysis.
In [7]:
absolute_difference_image = cv2.absdiff(base, dull)

plt.imshow(absolute_difference_image)
plt.axis('off')
plt.show()
  1. Multiplication: Multiplying an image by a scalar or another image scales the pixel values, adjusting brightness and contrast. This operation can enhance features or apply filters. Extreme multipliers may cause pixel saturation, which can lead to loss of detail or distortion.
In [8]:

plt.axis('on')
plt.show()
  1. Division: This operation divides the pixel values of one image by those of another, or by a scalar, to normalize or adjust contrast. It is essential to avoid division by zero, which can create artifacts or undefined values.

CONCLUSION

In this experiment, we explored basic image arithmetic operations: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and absolute difference. Addition can brighten images but may lead to overflow. Subtraction highlights differences, though negative values need careful management. Multiplication adjusts brightness and contrast, with a risk of saturation. Division normalizes contrast but requires avoiding division by zero. Absolute difference reveals pixel-wise variations without negative values. Each operation serves specific purposes and demands careful handling to ensure image quality.
